Padma Shri M C Dathan

Indian Space Scientist

M.C. Dathan is a renowned Indian space scientist who has made significant contributions to the development of India's space program. He has served in various capacities at the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), and is best known for his role as the director of the Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (VSSC) from 2007 to 2014. During his tenure at VSSC, Dathan oversaw the development and launch of several important satellites, including the PSLV C25 mission that sent India's Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM) spacecraft into orbit around Mars. This mission was a major achievement for india's space program, and helped to establish the country as a serious player in the global space industry. Dathan has received many awards and honors for his contributions to science and technology, including the Padma Shri in 2013. He continues to be an influential figure in the Indian scientific community, and his work has inspired many young people to pursue careers in space science and technology
